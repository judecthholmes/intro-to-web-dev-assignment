The Members

Five musicians from New York City who became one of the most important rock bands of the 2000s.

Band Members

Julian Casablancas

Lead Vocals

Born August 23, 1978, Julian is the frontman and primary songwriter of The Strokes. His distinctive vocal style, often processed through effects, became a signature sound of the band. He also leads the side project The Voidz.

  • Primary lyricist
  • Founded The Voidz in 2013
  • Son of Elite Model Management founder John Casablancas

Nick Valensi

Lead Guitar

Born January 16, 1981, Nick is known for his intricate guitar work and melodic solos. His playing style combines elements of punk, new wave, and classic rock.

  • Known for distinctive guitar tone
  • Member of side project CRX
  • Met Julian Casablancas in high school

Albert Hammond Jr.

Rhythm Guitar

Born April 9, 1980, Albert is the son of singer-songwriter Albert Hammond. His rhythm guitar work provides the foundation for The Strokes' sound, and he has released several successful solo albums.

  • Four solo albums released
  • Known for energetic stage presence
  • Contributes backing vocals

Nikolai Fraiture

Bass Guitar

Born November 13, 1978, Nikolai provides the driving bass lines that anchor The Strokes' sound. His minimalist approach to bass playing perfectly complements the band's garage rock aesthetic.

  • Childhood friend of Julian Casablancas
  • Released solo music as Nickel Eye
  • Known for steady, reliable bass lines

Fabrizio Moretti

Drums

Born June 2, 1980, in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, Fabrizio brings a unique rhythmic sensibility to the band. His drumming style is characterized by tight, precise beats that drive the band's energetic sound.

  • Born in Brazil, raised in New York
  • Member of side project Little Joy
  • Visual artist and painter

Band History

Formation (1998-2000)

The Strokes formed in 1998 when Julian Casablancas, who had been writing songs since his teens, recruited his friends to form a band. The original lineup came together through various connections at the Dwight School in Manhattan.

Breakthrough (2001-2003)

The band's debut album "Is This It" was released in 2001 and immediately garnered critical acclaim. The album's raw, stripped-down sound was a breath of fresh air in an era dominated by nu-metal and pop music.

Continued Success (2003-Present)

The Strokes have continued to evolve their sound over six studio albums, winning a Grammy Award in 2021 for "The New Abnormal" and cementing their legacy as one of the most important rock bands of their generation.
